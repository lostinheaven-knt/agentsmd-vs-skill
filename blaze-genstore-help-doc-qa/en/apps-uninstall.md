# Uninstall apps

In Genstore, you can easily manage your installed apps, including uninstalling apps you no longer need, reinstalling previously uninstalled apps, and reviewing your app's installation and uninstallation history.

## Uninstall an app

You can uninstall apps directly from the **Apps** page in your Genstore admin. Keep in mind that some apps may modify your store templates. Before uninstalling such apps, make sure you check if there are any additional steps required. If you have questions, contact the app developer for support via the **Apps** page.

### Steps to uninstall:

1. Log in to your Genstore admin and go to the **Apps** page.
2. Find the app you want to uninstall, click the **...** (more options) next to the app, and select **Uninstall app**.
3. (Optional) Choose a reason for uninstalling from the dropdown menu.
4. Click **Uninstall** to remove the app from your store.

## View uninstalled apps

You can view both installed and uninstalled apps on the **Apps** page in Genstore.

### Steps:

1. In your Genstore admin, go to the **Apps** page.
2. Click the **Uninstalled** tab to see a list of uninstalled apps.

## Reinstall an uninstalled app

You can reinstall apps you've uninstalled earlier, and view details like how many times the app was installed or uninstalled.

### Steps to reinstall:

1. In your Genstore admin, go to the **Apps** page.
2. Click the **Uninstalled** tab and find the app you want to reinstall.
3. Click **Install** to reinstall the app.

## View app installation or uninstallation history

You can track the full history of your apps in the Genstore admin, including both installation and uninstallation events.

### Steps to view history:

1. In your Genstore admin, go to the **Apps** page.
2. Click the **Installed** or **Uninstalled** tab to find the app.
3. Click the app's name to open its details page.
4. In the **App history** section of the app's details page, you can view both installation and uninstallation events.
   - If there are more than three events, click **View all** to see the full timeline.
   - Click on any event in the timeline to view more details, such as the uninstallation reason, description, and the person who performed the action.
